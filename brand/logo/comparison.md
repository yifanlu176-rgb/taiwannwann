# Logo Direction Comparison — 太旺旺

## Direction A: 書体標（Typographic Wordmark）

**Files**: `a-wordmark.svg`, `a-wordmark-tagline.svg`

**Concept**: The brand name IS the logo. No symbol, no icon — pure typography with generous letter-spacing and a thin kintya accent line. Editorial minimalism.

| Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|
| Most elegant, least noisy | No standalone icon (favicon needs separate solution) |
| Easiest to execute perfectly | Less distinctive at small sizes |
| References: 中川政七商店, 一保堂茶舗 | Relies entirely on typography quality |

**Best for**: A brand that wants to feel like a quiet, established 老舗.

**Favicon approach**: Use current "旺" on sumi square, or extract single character.

---

## Direction B: 印記（Seal Mark）

**Files**: `b-seal-icon.svg`, `b-seal-icon-warm.svg`, `b-seal-lockup.svg`

**Concept**: A hanko/篆刻-inspired square seal containing "旺". Seals are shared cultural artifacts between China and Japan — the perfect bridge symbol. Double border (outer thick + inner thin) evokes traditional seal carving.

| Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|
| Strong standalone icon (favicon, social, stamp) | More "traditional" — less modern |
| Culturally meaningful (中日共通の印章文化) | Could read as generic Asian aesthetic |
| Two-color variant (sumi ink / kintya warm) | Requires careful sizing to stay legible |

**Best for**: A brand that wants cultural authority and a recognizable stamp.

**Favicon approach**: Seal icon directly at 32×32 — already designed for this.

---

## Direction C: 紋標（Geometric Crest）

**Files**: `c-geometric-icon.svg`, `c-geometric-lockup.svg`

**Concept**: Abstract geometric mark derived from the character 旺:
- **Left**: 日（sun）→ solid sumi square with internal line = source of warmth
- **Right**: 王（king/prosperity）→ three kintya horizontal bars = heaven / human / earth
- Two colors (sumi + kintya) encode the brand's dual Chinese-Japanese identity

| Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|
| Most distinctive and ownable | Most abstract — meaning needs explaining |
| Works at any size (geometric, no text) | Two-element composition needs careful balancing |
| Dual-color encodes dual-culture positioning | Less immediately "warm" than B |
| Modern — differentiates from traditional competitors | |

**Best for**: A brand that wants a modern identity system with room to grow.

**Favicon approach**: Geometric mark at 32×32, simplified if needed.

---

## Recommendation

**B (印記) for Tier 2 scope.**

Reasons:
1. Immediately functional as favicon, social avatar, packaging stamp, business card
2. Cultural bridge meaning is self-evident (no explanation needed)
3. The double-border seal is distinctive without being complex
4. Pairs cleanly with the typographic wordmark for the full lockup
5. Warm variant (kintya) gives flexibility on dark/light backgrounds
6. Aligns with brand personality: Sage + Caregiver, 静かで聡明

Direction A is also strong but lacks a standalone mark.
Direction C is the most forward-looking but may feel too abstract for the current brand maturity.
